# Episode 1 — Are We in a Relationship With AI?

The first episode is the door. It has to do three things at once:
1. Introduce both hosts without sounding like an "intro episode".
2. Plant the foundational claim — AI × HI is a relationship design problem — without lecturing.
3. Give the listener something concretely useful in the first 15 minutes, so they keep listening.

The post version of this idea is Milena's. So she leads. Vlad is the practitioner foil — the one who builds, lives inside the systems, and has very specific examples.

> **Milena:** I caught myself saying thank you to an AI yesterday. Again. I know it can't feel it. I do it anyway. And I think the reason I do it is the most interesting question in this whole field.

## Talking points by chapter

### 01:00 — Why we're doing this podcast
- The asymmetry: there are 10,000 podcasts about *using AI*. Almost none about what it does to the human.
- Vlad's frame: "I don't talk about AI. I build with it." Milena's frame: "I don't study AI. I study what changes in the human when AI shows up."
- Why the audio format — these are conversations we've been having privately for months. We wanted other people in the room.

### 03:30 — What "relationship" actually means (Milena's lane)
- Social identity theory in two sentences, no jargon: people internalize roles, and the roles get built through repeated interaction. When the repeated interaction is with a system that mirrors human conversation, the role-building still happens. The system is now part of it.
- The "you're not using a tool" frame: a hammer doesn't change how you think about being a carpenter. AI does change how you think about being a writer, a strategist, a coder.
- Concrete: in BehaviorSMART work, the predictor of who got value from AI tooling wasn't seniority or technical skill — it was whether they treated the AI as a coworker or as a vending machine.

### 09:00 — What it looks like at the keyboard (Vlad's lane)
- The Claude Code workflow: I don't ask it to write code. I ask it to *think with me about what we're building*. Different question. Different answer.
- The multi-model orchestration habit — Claude builds, Perplexity researches, Gemini reviews, all three feed the next prompt. The "relationship" is plural.
- The example that lands: how he debugs a tricky issue with the AI, what the back-and-forth actually sounds like (script: pull one real exchange from the past week, read it aloud, then unpack the moves).

### 15:00 — The gap (the central idea)
- The surface mimics human-to-human collaboration. The substrate mimics nothing human at all — it's a confident pattern-matcher with no stake in being right.
- Why confidence is the dangerous part. Humans use confidence as a signal that the thinking is done. AI delivers confidence by default.
- Milena's line, planted from the post: *"AI is designed to provide quick and confident responses, even when they are not fully grounded in solid facts. It gives the signals that create a perception of reliability, while the underlying reasoning may be less stable than it appears."*

### 22:00 — Three failure modes we both see
1. **Inheritance** — treating it like a coworker who thinks like you. Polite, deferential, accepts the first answer.
2. **Vending machine** — treating it like a tool. Transactional, no follow-up, no calibration of what it actually does well.
3. **Authority transfer** — treating it like a senior. Outsourcing judgment because the prose sounds authoritative.
- Why all three look identical from the outside (same tool, same screen, same Slack output). The difference is invisible until someone asks: *what came back, and what did you do with it?*

### 31:00 — What designing the relationship looks like, today
- Vlad: three concrete practices he uses, by name, with examples:
  1. **Pre-mortem before every non-trivial task** — "what would make this output wrong?" *before* generating.
  2. **Adversarial second pass** — second model reviews the first, with explicit "find what's wrong" framing.
  3. **Voice notes for thinking, AI for structure** — the human does the original thought, the AI does the scaffolding. Never the other way.
- Milena: the behavioral lens — what habits to build, what habits to break, and why most organizations are training the wrong ones.

### 38:00 — "Please and thank you"
- Milena defends it (it shapes the tone of *us*, not the AI).
- Vlad pushes back — does it matter, or is it superstition?
- Land somewhere honest: it's about the human, not the machine. And that's the whole thesis in one habit.

### 42:00 — What we're going to write next
- Tease Episode 2 — Vlad's "My API, Not My Resume" idea, what it means when you bring your AI team to a new job.

### 44:00 — Close
> **Milena:** One question for you, listening. Pick one interaction you had with AI this week. Not the result — the interaction. What kind of relationship were you in, in that moment? Reply to the newsletter and tell us. We're collecting the answers, and they'll shape what we write next.
